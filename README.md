# space-invaders
Everybody needs their own space invaders game


Space Invaders isn't just a classic; from an engineering and historical perspective, it is arguably the most important video game ever made. It single-handedly transitioned the industry from the "Pong era" into the Golden Age of Arcades.

1. The Creator: Tomohiro Nishikado


The game was developed in 1978 by Tomohiro Nishikado at Taito. Unlike modern games made by teams of hundreds, Nishikado did nearly everything himself: the game design, the pixel art, the sound, and even the custom hardware.

Because Japan lacked powerful microcomputers at the time, he had to build his own development board and a custom motherboard based on the Intel 8080 CPU. The original game was actually in black and white; the "color" seen in later arcade cabinets was often just colored cellophane strips stuck onto the monitor.

2. A Famous Engineering "Glitch"


One of the most iconic features of the game—the way the aliens speed up as you kill them—was actually an accidental side effect of the hardware.


- The Logic: The 8080 CPU was underpowered. When the screen was full of 55 aliens, the processor struggled to render and move all the sprites, causing them to move slowly.

- The Result: As the player destroyed aliens, the CPU had fewer sprites to calculate. This allowed the loop to run faster, causing the remaining enemies to accelerate.

- The Decision: Nishikado realized this created a natural "difficulty curve" and decided to keep it as a gameplay mechanic rather than trying to fix it.

3. Design Inspiration

- The Enemies: Nishikado originally wanted to use human soldiers as enemies, but Taito was concerned that shooting people might be seen as immoral. He tried tanks and planes, but they didn't look right. Inspired by H.G. Wells' The War of the Worlds and the popularity of Star Wars, he eventually settled on space aliens modeled after sea creatures (squids, crabs, and octopuses).

- Breakout: The game was partially inspired by Atari’s Breakout. Nishikado wanted to create a version where the "blocks" fought back.
